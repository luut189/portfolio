# Product

## Register

brand

## Users
[This portfolio is for a broad public audience, including recruiters, hiring managers, collaborators, peers, and anyone evaluating Tuong Luu's work. They arrive to quickly understand who he is, what he has built, and whether his taste, technical depth, and personality stand out from other candidates and creators.]

## Product Purpose
[This site exists to showcase Tuong Luu through selected work, experience, and contact paths in a way that feels personal and memorable. Success means visitors can scan the portfolio quickly, trust the technical credibility behind the work, and leave with a clear sense that the site could only belong to him.]

## Brand Personality
[Personal, sharp, distinct. The tone should feel technically credible and sparse, with enough playful detail to avoid sterility. It should present confidence without sounding corporate or over-produced.]

## Anti-references
[Do not let this feel generic, SaaS-like, template-heavy, corporate, or interchangeable with a startup landing page. Avoid polished-but-anonymous portfolio patterns that prioritize trendiness over identity. The site should not feel like a product marketing surface disguised as a personal portfolio.]

## Design Principles
[Keep the existing visual character unless there is a strong reason to change it. Let restraint do the work: sparse layouts, concise copy, and high-signal content should carry the experience. Prove technical depth through real work, not decorative claims. Use playful details selectively so they feel authored, not gimmicky. Every major surface should reinforce that this portfolio is specific to Tuong Luu, not a reusable template.]

## Accessibility & Inclusion
[Follow normal modern accessibility expectations: solid keyboard navigation, readable contrast, clear focus states, semantic structure, and restrained motion by default. Preserve clarity and usability across devices without adding unnecessary visual noise.]
